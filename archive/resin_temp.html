Project: Big Knob Jan/Feb 2019

I saw a cool video (link here) of a simple wood+resin pour project. There are plenty of similar craft videos out there, but I like this guy. The idea is, you break off a piece of wood to a live, jagged edge, pour resin over it, and shape and polish the piece as a whole to your whims.

I decided to give it a quick shot. Made a couple decorative test pieces, then hit upon an idea for a grander project: a new shift knob for my Trooper.

First thing to do: get a decent-sized chunk of nice wood (big enough to cut down to an approximately knob-sized diameter), and break off a piece. This actually gave me a bit of trouble: with my smaller tests, I had used a vise to apply pressure and snap a piece "cleanly" down the middle.

That certainly wasn't possibly with this hunkin' chunk of wood-- way too big. My next idea was to prop it up on a couple rocks in my backyard and smash a sledgehammer clean through it (Protip-safety glasses). Hey, even if it didn't work, it's always fun to swing a bigass hammer at stuff.

Well, after smashing a few rocks to smithereens and driving a couple more headfirst into the dirt, I was finally able to get a workable piece. After a quick drilled hole, it was ready to pop on the gear selector for an initial look.


After the initial installation, it was time to do the thing for real. I chopped off the excess wood on the bottom, mixed up the resin (simple 1:1 ratio + some colorant), and waited for my creation to harden. 24 hours later, the rough shape was ready to start working on.


Sanding. So much sanding. The belt sander makes short work of wood and resin alike; pictured is the knob after roughing out the facets. Making flat faces, rather than a contoured form, makes it easier to control the next step: hand sanding. Working on the dining table with some choice jams on in the background, it's a labor of love: working each face up through finer grit sandpaper until I got the silky-smooth product I was looking for.

Seriously, touching this thing after 2000-grit passes felt gooood.


Endgame. A couple layers of polyurethane to seal it, plus some polishing, and it was ready to pop off. A quick hole in the bottom, and we're ready for install!

Crappy post-install pic. Did I mention it glows in the dark? Neat!
